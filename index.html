Why the world needs a procurement Pulse

A school district vetting an AI tutor today reads a 40-page vendor whitepaper, a HIPAA attestation, and a marketing PDF. A hospital vetting a clinical-decision-support tool does the same. A federal agency does the same. Every buyer reinvents the same review wheel because vendors don't disclose in a comparable format.

The Suite ships an open substrate that makes disclosure machine-readable. The Pulse measures who's using it — and, more importantly, who isn't. Vendors that publish appear on the public scoreboard. Vendors that don't appear on the same scoreboard with a blank cell. Buyers cite the report. Regulators reference it. The race-to-the-top mechanism has, until now, been missing.

For buyers

One canonical place to compare vendor disclosure posture before procurement starts. Filter by vertical (EdTech, HealthTech, FinTech), by required field (HIPAA, FDA SaMD, FERPA), by signature status.

For vendors

Quarterly visibility into your peers' disclosure posture. If your direct competitors are publishing their Agent Cards and you aren't, the Pulse makes that gap legible to your prospects.

For regulators

An evidence base for what self-disclosure can and cannot achieve in the AI market, refreshed every ninety days. Comparable across verticals, replicable methodology, no vendor cooperation required.

The methodology, end-to-end

Every step is open-source. Every data point is publicly fetchable. The Pulse is reproducible by anyone who runs the same crawl against the same universe of domains on the same day.

  1. Universe selection. Issue #1: Fortune 500 + top 100 K-12 EdTech vendors (from EdTech Top 40 lists) + top 50 HealthTech AI vendors (FDA-cleared SaMD + clinical-decision-support market). ~1,200 domains. Published as universe.csv alongside the issue.
  2. Crawl. aeo-crawler against each domain's /.well-known/ for the eleven Suite documents. JSON Lines output, hashed for replay.
  3. Validate. Every fetched document goes through aeo-validator-service. Invalid documents are flagged but counted as published for the publication-rate numerator.
  4. Signature check. If a document references a signing_key_url, hash-attestation-rs verifies the ed25519 signature against the canonical-JSON hash.
  5. Drift detection. Each domain's documents are re-fetched fourteen days into the quarter. Any document whose content_hash changes without a corresponding version bump is logged as a drift event.
  6. Reporting. Aggregated metrics (publication rate, validity rate, signature rate, drift rate) by vertical, by spec, by signed-status. Per-vendor rows go into the premium edition; public edition reports aggregate-only.

The crawl runs from a US-East datacenter with a standard user-agent (kineticgain-pulse-crawler/1.0), respects robots.txt, rate-limits to 10 req/s per origin, and identifies itself with a contact email in the User-Agent string. No authentication. No headless browser. No vendor cooperation needed.
