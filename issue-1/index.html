ISSUE #1 · MAY 2026 · THE ZERO BASELINE

Nobody publishes machine-readable AI governance disclosure yet. Including us.

The first real crawl of the AI Procurement Pulse is in. We probed 37 vendor domains across 9 verticals for all eleven Kinetic Gain Protocol Suite documents at their /.well-known/ paths. The result is unambiguous — and honest.

0.0%
of 37 domains publish any Suite document · average disclosure score 0 / 100

This is the starting line, not a verdict

The Suite shipped in 2025. Machine-readable AI vendor disclosure is a brand-new practice, and a brand-new practice begins at zero. Issue #1 exists to calibrate the instrument and mark that zero — so every future issue can measure the climb against a real, reproducible baseline rather than a vibe.

We held ourselves to the same ruler. Kinetic Gain's own properties — kineticgain.com, suite.kineticgain.com, clinical.kineticgain.com, decisions.kineticgain.com — are in the universe, and they score zero too. You can't run a disclosure index where the author is exempt. Dogfooding our own specs is the first item on the Issue #2 docket.

Methodology integrity note. The zero is real, not a probe artifact. The crawler is discriminator-aware — a 200 OK serving unrelated JSON at a well-known path is not counted as a published spec. Every domain genuinely publishes nothing at these paths today.

What changes this number

Adoption climbs when buyers start asking for it. A district that publishes an AI Procurement Decision Card forces the vendors it reviews to publish the declarations that card cites. The fastest path off zero is procurement pressure, not vendor goodwill.

Roadmap. Issue #2 expands the universe toward the full ~1,200-domain target (Fortune 500 + top 100 K-12 EdTech + top 50 HealthTech AI), adds signature verification, and reports the first drift deltas. And it will show Kinetic Gain's own properties as the first non-zero row — we publish before we ask anyone else to.
